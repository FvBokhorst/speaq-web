SPEAQ
A Quantum-Resistant Communication and Value Network
"By the people, for the people."
Version 2.0 - April 2026

SPEAQ is a communication and value transfer network built on post-quantum cryptography, designed for a world where privacy is not a privilege but a right. It combines end-to-end encrypted messaging, voice and video calling, and a native digital currency (Q-Credits) in a single platform that requires no registration, no phone number, and no bank account.


SPEAQ introduces Proof of Contribution (DPoC), a consensus mechanism where participants earn their position by helping the network - not by solving puzzles or staking capital. The result is a system where value flows from participation, not from wealth.


This paper describes the architecture, the economics, and the principles behind SPEAQ.

Section 01

Why SPEAQ Exists

1.1 The Question

SPEAQ was born from a simple question: why should anyone have the power to decide whether two people can talk to each other?

Not a government. Not a corporation. Not an algorithm. No one.

If you have someone you love in another country, in another timezone, under another regime - you should be able to speak with them. Always. Without permission. Without surveillance. Without the possibility that someone, somewhere, flips a switch and silences you both.

This is not a theoretical concern. It happens today. Entire countries are disconnected from the global internet. Messaging platforms are blocked. Calls are monitored. People lose contact with their families, their partners, their communities - not because of technology, but because of power.

SPEAQ exists because we refuse to accept that.

1.2 The Bigger Problem

There are 5.4 billion people connected to the internet. Most of them use communication platforms built by companies whose business model depends on collecting data, serving advertisements, or complying with government requests to access private conversations.

At the same time, 1.4 billion adults worldwide have no bank account. They cannot send or receive money without intermediaries who charge fees, impose limits, or deny service entirely.

These are not separate problems. They are the same problem: people depend on systems they do not control.

When a platform decides to shut down your account, your messages disappear. When a bank decides you are too risky, your money is frozen. When a government decides your communication is inconvenient, your app is blocked.

1.3 What We Believe

We believe that the ability to speak freely and transact freely are foundational human capabilities. Not features. Not services. Capabilities.

A capability cannot be granted by a company or a government. It can only be built into the architecture of the system itself. If a system CAN be shut down, it eventually WILL be shut down - by policy, by pressure, or by economics.

SPEAQ is designed so that no single entity - including its creators - can read your messages, freeze your funds, or exclude you from the network.

This is not a policy. It is mathematics.

1.4 Who SPEAQ Is For

SPEAQ is for everyone. But some people need it more than others:

  • The 1.4 billion unbanked - who need a way to send and receive value without a bank account
  • People in restrictive environments - who need communication that cannot be monitored or blocked
  • Communities building local economies - who need a currency that rewards participation, not speculation
  • Anyone who values privacy - who wants communication that is private by design, not by promise
Section 02

Principles

2.1 No Registration

SPEAQ does not ask for your name, email, phone number, or any identifier. Your identity is a cryptographic keypair generated on your device. You are known only by your SPEAQ ID - a string of characters that is not linked to anything in the physical world.

Why this matters: registration creates a database. A database can be breached, subpoenaed, or sold. No registration means no database. No database means nothing to breach.

2.2 Zero Knowledge

The SPEAQ relay server operates on a zero-knowledge principle. It sees only encrypted data moving between addresses. It cannot:

  • Read messages
  • Identify senders or receivers
  • Access wallet balances
  • Log communication patterns

The server is a utility, like a road. It carries traffic. It does not read the letters.

2.3 Sovereignty

Your keys, your messages, your money - all stored on your device. SPEAQ cannot access them, freeze them, or take them away. If you lose your keys, they are gone. This is the price of sovereignty, and it is worth paying.

2.4 Contribution Over Capital

In most systems, the people with the most money have the most power. SPEAQ inverts this: the people who contribute the most to the network earn the most. Your value comes from what you do, not what you have.

3.4 Sovereign Wallet

Transactions are signed on-device and submitted directly to the blockchain node. No intermediary holds your keys or can authorize transactions on your behalf.

3.6 Network Layer

SPEAQ nodes communicate using libp2p, the same peer-to-peer networking stack used by Polkadot, Filecoin, and IPFS:

  • GossipSub - Efficient broadcast of blocks and transactions to all peers
  • Kademlia DHT - Decentralised peer discovery, no central server needed
  • Noise Protocol - All node-to-node connections are encrypted
  • BLE Mesh - For environments without internet, relay messages over Bluetooth

3.7 On-Chain Transactions

The complete transaction flow:

  1. Sender creates transaction JSON (from, to, amount, timestamp)
  2. Sender signs with ML-DSA-65 (FIPS 204) sovereign keys
  3. Transaction submitted via POST /api/transaction to speaq-chain
  4. Node verifies ML-DSA-65 signature against public key
  5. Node verifies sender address = SHA-256(publicKey) with SQ1 prefix
  6. Transaction enters mempool
  7. Next block producer (every 30 seconds) drains mempool into block
  8. Block signed with ML-DSA-65 (primary) + SPHINCS+ (backup)
  9. Block stored in RocksDB, broadcast to peers via GossipSub
Section 04

Proof of Contribution (DPoC)

4.1 The Problem with Existing Consensus

Proof of Work (Bitcoin) secures the network through computational puzzles. This works, but at enormous environmental cost. The Bitcoin network consumes more electricity than many countries. The security comes from waste.

Proof of Stake (Ethereum) replaces computation with capital. Validators stake money to earn the right to produce blocks. This is more efficient, but it concentrates power with those who already have wealth. The rich get richer.

Both systems answer the question "who should produce the next block?" with a proxy: either "whoever has the most hardware" or "whoever has the most money."

4.2 A Different Answer

SPEAQ asks a simpler question: who has helped the network the most?

ContributionWhat You DoWhy It Matters
Message relayForward encrypted messages between usersKeeps the network connected
Proof validationVerify blocks produced by other validatorsEnsures network integrity
Data storageStore blockchain data for other nodesPreserves the shared history
Mesh networkingBridge connections for users without internetExtends access to underserved areas
TranslationTranslate the app into new languagesMakes SPEAQ accessible to more people
User onboardingHelp new users join the networkGrows the community

Important: All contribution verification and reward calculation happens server-side. The app does not mine on your device. It earns rewards by participating in the network, and the server validates and assigns those rewards. Your phone's battery and performance are not affected.

4.3 Geographic Diversity

To prevent concentration in wealthy regions, SPEAQ applies a geographic diversity bonus:

  • Nodes in Africa receive a 150% score multiplier
  • South America: 140%
  • Oceania: 130%
  • Asia: 110%
  • Europe and North America: 100% (baseline)

No more than 5 of the 21 validators can come from the same geographic region.

4.4 How Block Production Works

Every 30 seconds, the protocol deterministically selects one validator to produce the next block. The selection is weighted by contribution score.

The selection is deterministic: every node can independently calculate who should produce each block, using only the block height and the previous block's hash. No communication is needed to agree on whose turn it is.

When a validator produces a block, the block is:

  1. Signed with ML-DSA-65 (primary quantum-resistant signature)
  2. Counter-signed with SPHINCS+ (backup hash-based signature)
  3. Broadcast to all peers via GossipSub
  4. Validated by receiving nodes (signature, merkle root, chain link, height)
  5. Stored in each node's local RocksDB database

4.5 Finality

A block achieves finality when 2/3 of the active validators (14 out of 21) confirm it. Once final, a block cannot be reversed.

7.2 Transaction Privacy

SPEAQ transactions are signed with ML-DSA-65 (FIPS 204) quantum-resistant signatures. The transaction flow is designed for privacy:

  • Sovereign keys: Your signing keys are generated on your device and never leave it
  • Pseudonymous addresses: Your on-chain address (SQ1 prefix) is derived from a SHA-256 hash of your public key - not linked to your identity
  • No metadata on relay: The relay server cannot see transaction amounts, sender, or recipient
  • End-to-end signed: Every transaction is cryptographically signed and verified by the blockchain network

Advanced transaction privacy features (ring signatures, stealth addresses, zero-knowledge proofs) are planned for a future protocol upgrade.
